# Nasi Goreng

*Indonesia's national fried rice — smoky, sweet, savoury, and completely addictive.*

---

## About this dish

Nasi goreng is the Indonesian version of fried rice, distinguished by kecap manis (sweet soy sauce) and terasi (shrimp paste), which give it a deeply caramelised, complex flavour unlike any other fried rice. It is a dish built for leftover rice — freshly cooked rice is too wet and will not fry properly.

## Ingredients

**Essential:**
- 300g cold cooked rice (day-old is ideal) — essential
- 2 tbsp kecap manis (Indonesian sweet soy sauce) — essential
- 1 tsp terasi/belacan (shrimp paste), toasted — essential for authenticity; substitute 1 tsp fish sauce
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 shallots, finely sliced
- 1–2 red chillies, sliced (or 1 tsp sambal oelek)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 eggs (for frying)

**Optional:**
- 100g prawns, chicken, or tofu
- 1 tbsp regular soy sauce

**To serve:**
- Fried egg (sunny side up)
- Cucumber slices
- Crispy shallots
- Lime wedge
- Prawn crackers

---

## Method

1. If using terasi, wrap in foil and toast over a flame or in a dry pan for 1 minute per side.
2. Heat wok over highest heat. Add oil. Fry shallots and garlic for 1 minute until fragrant and lightly golden.
3. Add chilli and toasted terasi. Mash and stir into the oil for 30 seconds.
4. Add protein if using. Cook for 2 minutes.
5. Add cold rice. Break up any clumps. Toss continuously for 2 minutes over high heat.
6. Add kecap manis and soy sauce. Toss vigorously for another 1–2 minutes until the rice is evenly coated, slightly caramelised, and fragrant.
7. Taste and adjust seasoning. Plate and top with a sunny-side-up fried egg, cucumber, crispy shallots, and a lime wedge.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Too sweet?** Add a splash of soy sauce or fish sauce to balance.
- **Lacks depth?** A little more toasted terasi or a dash of fish sauce.
- **Rice clumping?** The wok was not hot enough — use higher heat and keep tossing.

---

## Make it yours

- **Nasi goreng kampung:** Add anchovies (ikan bilis) fried until crispy at the start.
- **Vegetarian version:** Skip terasi and use soy sauce; add extra vegetables.
- Add frozen peas or finely sliced cabbage for a vegetable boost.

---

## The next day

Nasi goreng is best fresh. Leftover nasi goreng can be re-fried the next day with a little oil.

---

## Food safety note

Ensure leftover rice was cooled quickly and stored in the fridge within 1 hour of cooking. Never leave cooked rice at room temperature for more than 2 hours.
