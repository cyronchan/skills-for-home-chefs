# Crispy Roast Chicken Thighs

*The home cook's most reliable weeknight win.*

---

## About this dish

Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs are the best cut for home roasting: more forgiving than breast, more flavourful, cheaper, and almost impossible to overcook. The method here produces reliably crispy skin and juicy meat with minimal effort. Once you have the base technique, you can season it any way you like.

## Ingredients

**Essential:**
- 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
- 1 tbsp neutral oil or olive oil
- 1 tsp salt
- ½ tsp black pepper

**Base seasoning (choose one or mix):**
- Classic: garlic powder, paprika, dried thyme
- Asian: five spice, garlic, ginger, soy sauce
- Lemon herb: lemon zest, rosemary, garlic
- Spicy: paprika, cumin, chilli flakes, garlic

---

## Method

1. Take chicken out of the fridge 20 minutes before cooking. Pat the skin thoroughly dry with paper towels — moisture is the enemy of crispy skin.
2. Preheat oven to 220°C / 200°C fan.
3. Rub chicken all over with oil, then season generously with salt, pepper, and chosen spices. Make sure to season under the skin where possible.
4. Place skin-side up on a rack over a baking tray, or directly on a baking tray. Do not crowd the pieces.
5. Roast for 35–40 minutes until skin is deep golden and crispy.
6. Check doneness: pierce the thickest part near the bone — juices should run completely clear, or internal temperature reaches 74°C / 165°F.
7. Rest for 5 minutes before serving.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Skin not crispy enough?** The skin was wet, or the oven was not hot enough. Finish under the grill for 2–3 minutes.
- **Seasoning lacking?** A squeeze of lemon and a flake of salt added after resting makes a big difference.
- **Pan juices?** Do not discard — deglaze with a splash of wine or stock and a knob of butter for an instant sauce.

---

## Make it yours

- Toss vegetables (potatoes, courgette, cherry tomatoes) in the same seasoning and roast alongside on the same tray.
- Marinate overnight in yogurt, garlic, and spices for a more tender, deeply flavoured result.
- Rest the cooked chicken in a pan sauce of butter, garlic, and fresh herbs for extra richness.

---

## The next day

Keeps in the fridge for 3 days. Cold roast chicken thighs are excellent sliced over salads, torn into noodle soup, or eaten in a sandwich. Reheat in a hot oven or air fryer to revive the crispy skin.

---

## Food safety note

Internal temperature must reach 74°C / 165°F. Do not serve pink chicken. Wash hands and surfaces after handling raw poultry.
