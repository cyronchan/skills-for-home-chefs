# Simple Vinaigrette

*The most transferable sauce in Western cooking. Learn the ratio; use it forever.*

---

## About this dish

A vinaigrette is an emulsion of oil and acid, balanced with salt and usually a little mustard as an emulsifier. The classic ratio is 3 parts oil to 1 part acid. Once you understand this, you can make a dressing from almost any oil and any acid in your kitchen. It takes 2 minutes and makes any salad significantly better than a bottled dressing.

## Ingredients

**Essential:**
- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tbsp red wine vinegar (or lemon juice)
- ½ tsp Dijon mustard (emulsifier — holds oil and acid together)
- Pinch of salt
- Pinch of sugar or ½ tsp honey

**Optional:**
- 1 small clove garlic, minced or grated
- Fresh or dried herbs (thyme, tarragon, chives)
- Black pepper

---

## Method

1. Place mustard, vinegar, salt, and sugar in a small bowl or jar.
2. Whisk or shake to combine.
3. Add olive oil slowly while whisking continuously — or add all at once to a jar and shake vigorously for 30 seconds.
4. Taste. Adjust: more acid if flat, more oil if too sharp, more salt if under-seasoned.
5. Dress the salad just before serving — not in advance, or leaves will wilt.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Too sharp?** Add more oil or a pinch more sugar.
- **Too oily and flat?** Add more vinegar or a squeeze of lemon.
- **Not cohesive (oil separating)?** Add a little more mustard and shake again.
- **Missing something?** A tiny amount of good quality vinegar or a drop of honey often fixes it.

---

## Make it yours

- **Asian sesame dressing:** Sesame oil + rice vinegar + soy sauce + ginger + honey.
- **Tahini dressing:** Tahini + lemon + garlic + water to thin — excellent on roasted vegetables.
- **Balsamic:** Replace red wine vinegar with aged balsamic — sweeter and richer.

---

## The next day

Keeps in a jar in the fridge for 1 week. Oil may solidify — just bring to room temperature and shake. Use on grain bowls, roasted vegetables, cold proteins, or as a marinade.
