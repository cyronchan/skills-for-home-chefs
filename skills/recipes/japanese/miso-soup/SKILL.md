# Miso Soup 味噌汁

*The most important five-minute dish in Japanese home cooking.*

---

## About this dish

Miso soup is made fresh at almost every Japanese meal. It is fast, endlessly variable, and deeply restorative. The foundation is dashi — a simple stock of kombu and bonito flakes — which gives the soup its depth. The miso is added off the heat to preserve its living cultures and complex flavour.

## Ingredients

**For the dashi (makes 500ml):**
- 500ml cold water — essential
- 1 piece kombu (about 10cm square) — essential
- 10g katsuobushi (bonito flakes) — essential for full flavour; omit for vegan

**For the soup:**
- 1.5–2 tbsp white (shiro) or mixed miso paste — essential
- 100g silken or soft tofu, cubed — classic addition
- 1 sheet dried wakame seaweed (rehydrated) — classic addition
- 1 spring onion, finely sliced — to finish

**Substitutions:**
- Kombu dashi → instant dashi powder (dashi-no-moto) for a 1-minute version
- White miso → red miso for a deeper, saltier flavour
- Tofu and wakame → mushrooms, daikon, potato, clams, or any quick-cooking ingredient

---

## Method

1. **Make dashi.** Place kombu in cold water. Heat slowly over medium heat. Remove kombu just before the water boils (around 60°C / 140°F).
2. Bring to a boil. Add bonito flakes. Turn off heat immediately. Let steep for 3 minutes. Strain.
3. **Build the soup.** Return dashi to a gentle simmer. Add tofu cubes and rehydrated wakame.
4. **Dissolve miso off the heat.** Remove the pan from heat. Place miso in a small sieve or ladle, dip into the soup, and stir to dissolve. Never boil miso — it destroys flavour and beneficial cultures.
5. Taste and adjust — add a little more miso if needed.
6. Serve immediately, garnished with spring onion.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Too salty?** Add more dashi or a little hot water.
- **Too bland?** Add more miso, a few drops of soy sauce, or a small piece of kombu steeped longer.
- **Missing depth?** A few drops of mirin add sweetness and round the flavour.

---

## Make it yours

- **Clam miso soup:** Add fresh clams to the simmering dashi — they open in 2–3 minutes and add beautiful brininess.
- **Mushroom version:** Shiitake or enoki mushrooms add earthiness and make it fully vegan.
- **Hearty version:** Add potato, daikon, and fried tofu puffs (aburaage) for a filling meal.

---

## The next day

Miso soup is best made fresh. Leftover dashi keeps in the fridge for 3 days and can be used for noodle soups, sauces, or cooking rice.
