# Oyakodon 親子丼

*"Parent and child" — chicken and egg together over rice. One of Japan's greatest comfort bowls.*

---

## About this dish

Oyakodon means "parent and child" — the chicken and egg cooked together. It is one of Japan's most beloved weeknight bowls: a sweet-savoury dashi broth, tender chicken, and barely-set egg spooned over rice. The egg should be runny in the centre, like a soft custard. The whole dish comes together in under 10 minutes once you have cooked rice.

## Ingredients

**Main:**
- 2 chicken thighs (boneless, skin on or off), sliced — essential
- 3 eggs, lightly beaten — essential
- ½ onion, thinly sliced — essential
- Steamed Japanese short-grain rice for 2 — essential

**Broth:**
- 150ml dashi (or water + ½ tsp instant dashi powder)
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp mirin
- 1 tsp sake (optional)
- 1 tsp sugar

**To finish:**
- Mitsuba or spring onion, roughly chopped
- Shichimi togarashi (optional)

---

## Method

1. Combine dashi, soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar in a small frying pan or donburi pan. Bring to a simmer.
2. Add onion. Cook for 2–3 minutes until softened.
3. Add chicken pieces in a single layer. Simmer for 4–5 minutes until just cooked through.
4. Pour beaten egg evenly over the chicken. Do not stir.
5. Cover with a lid. Cook for 60–90 seconds over medium heat. The egg should be barely set on top, still runny in the centre.
6. Slide immediately over a bowl of hot rice. Garnish with mitsuba or spring onion.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Is the broth balanced?** It should be sweet, savoury, and slightly rich — not too salty or too sweet. Adjust soy and sugar.
- **Is the egg right?** The centre should be soft and custardy — err on the side of underdone; residual heat from the rice continues cooking it.

---

## Make it yours

- **Katsudon:** Replace chicken with a breaded pork cutlet (tonkatsu) for the other classic version.
- **Vegetarian:** Replace chicken with firm tofu or mushrooms.
- Add shichimi togarashi for heat and complexity.

---

## The next day

Best eaten fresh. Leftover broth is excellent as a noodle soup base.

---

## Food safety note

The egg is intentionally soft-set. Do not serve to vulnerable individuals who should avoid undercooked egg.
