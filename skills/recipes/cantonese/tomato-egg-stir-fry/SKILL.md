# Tomato and Egg Stir-fry 番茄炒蛋

*Arguably the most cooked dish in Chinese home kitchens. Everyone has their version.*

---

## About this dish

番茄炒蛋 is a dish that crosses regional boundaries across China and has a permanent place in every Cantonese home kitchen. It is fast, cheap, and deeply satisfying. The trick is in the sequencing: cook the egg first, remove it, build the tomato sauce, then reunite them at the end so the egg stays soft.

## Ingredients

**Main:**
- 3 eggs — essential
- 2 medium tomatoes (about 300g), cut into wedges — essential
- 2 cloves garlic, minced — essential
- 2 tbsp neutral oil — essential

**Seasoning:**
- 1 tsp sugar — essential (balances the tomato acidity)
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- ½ tsp sesame oil
- Salt to taste
- 2 tbsp water or stock

**To finish:**
- 1 spring onion, sliced

---

## Method

1. Beat eggs with a pinch of salt and ½ tsp sesame oil.
2. Heat wok over high heat. Add 1 tbsp oil. Pour in eggs and scramble gently until just set but still soft and slightly underdone. Remove and set aside.
3. Add remaining oil to the wok. Fry garlic for 15 seconds.
4. Add tomato wedges and stir-fry for 2 minutes until they begin to soften and release juice.
5. Add sugar, soy sauce, and water. Stir and cook for 1 more minute until a light sauce forms.
6. Return the eggs to the wok. Toss gently to combine — do not break up the egg too much. Cook for 30 seconds.
7. Taste, adjust seasoning, scatter spring onion, and serve immediately over rice.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Too sharp?** The tomatoes may be very acidic — add a little more sugar.
- **Too flat?** Add a few drops more soy sauce.
- **Egg too rubbery?** It was overcooked in step 2 — pull it off heat earlier next time; it will finish cooking when returned to the pan.

---

## Make it yours

- Add a small spoonful of tomato paste for a deeper, richer sauce.
- Add a pinch of white pepper to the egg for warmth.
- For a Shanghainese variation, add a splash of Shaoxing wine when frying the tomatoes.

---

## The next day

Keeps in the fridge for 1 day. Reheat gently in a pan. Excellent stirred into congee or served over noodles instead of rice.
