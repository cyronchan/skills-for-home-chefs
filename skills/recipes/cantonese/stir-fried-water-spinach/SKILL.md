# Stir-fried Water Spinach with Fermented Tofu 腒乳烙通菜

*One of Hong Kong’s most beloved wok dishes. Deceptively simple, deeply satisfying.*

---

## About this dish

腒乳烙通菜 is a staple of Cantonese home cooking and cha chaan tengs alike. The water spinach wilts quickly in a screaming-hot wok, the fermented tofu (nam yue or fu yue) melts into a creamy, pungent sauce, and the garlic adds punch. Done in under five minutes once your wok is hot. The challenge is getting the wok hot enough at home.

## Ingredients

**Main ingredients:**
- 400g water spinach (ong choy / 通菜) — essential; substitute morning glory or regular spinach
- 3–4 cloves garlic, roughly crushed — essential
- 1–2 cubes white fermented tofu (fu yue / 腈乳) — essential for authenticity; substitute 1 tsp white miso + ½ tsp rice vinegar
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (vegetable or lard for more flavour) — essential

**Seasoning:**
- ½ tsp sugar
- 1 tbsp water or Shaoxing wine
- Salt to taste

**Substitutions:**
- Water spinach → morning glory, regular spinach, or choy sum (reduce cooking time further)
- Fermented white tofu → fermented red tofu (nan yue) for a more intense, red colour and deeper flavour

---

## Method

1. **Prep the greens.** Wash the water spinach thoroughly. Tear or cut into 8–10cm lengths, separating the thicker stems from the leafy tops.

2. **Mash the tofu.** In a small bowl, mash 1–2 cubes of fermented tofu with a fork into a paste. Add the sugar and a splash of water or Shaoxing wine. Mix.

3. **Heat the wok.** Get your wok screaming hot over the highest heat your hob can produce. Add oil and let it shimmer and just begin to smoke.

4. **Fry the garlic.** Add the crushed garlic and toss for 15–20 seconds until fragrant. Do not let it burn.

5. **Add the stems first.** Add the thicker stems and toss vigorously for 30 seconds.

6. **Add the tofu paste.** Add the mashed tofu mixture and toss to coat everything.

7. **Add the leafy tops.** Add the leafy portion and toss continuously for 1–2 minutes until just wilted. The spinach should be bright green and slightly tender but not mushy.

8. **Taste and serve immediately.** Plate and eat right away — water spinach continues to wilt and release water if left to sit.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Is it savoury enough?** The fermented tofu carries most of the salt. Taste before adding any additional salt — it may not need any.
- **Is it bitter?** A pinch of sugar balances the slight bitterness of the greens and tofu.
- **Does it taste flat?** A very small splash of rice vinegar or a few drops of sesame oil at the finish adds lift.

---

## Make it yours

- **With chilli:** Add a sliced fresh red chilli with the garlic for heat.
- **With dried shrimp:** Toss in a small handful of dried shrimp with the garlic for a deeper, more complex flavour.
- **Simpler version:** Skip the fermented tofu and use just garlic, oil, and oyster sauce for a faster, milder version.

---

## The next day

This dish does not keep well — water spinach becomes watery and limp. Cook only what you will eat immediately. Leftovers can be added to congee or noodle soup.

---

## Food safety note

Ensure the greens are washed thoroughly, especially if bought from a wet market. Water spinach grown in water can carry soil and grit.

---

## Contributor notes

The single biggest home-cook obstacle is heat. A domestic gas hob works well. Induction can work but you need to allow longer preheating. The wok must be genuinely hot before the oil goes in — flick a drop of water in; it should evaporate instantly.
