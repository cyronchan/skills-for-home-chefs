# Ginger and Spring Onion Poached Chicken 薑蔥雞

*The dish that defines Cantonese flavour: clean protein, punchy sauce, good rice.*

---

## About this dish

薑蔥雞 is one of the foundational dishes of Cantonese cooking. The chicken is poached gently so it stays silky and just cooked, then finished with a hot ginger-spring onion oil that sizzles on contact. The poaching liquid becomes the soup or the base for cooking the rice. Nothing is wasted.

## Ingredients

**Main:**
- 2 chicken leg quarters or 4 bone-in thighs — essential
- 4 slices fresh ginger — essential
- 2 spring onions, tied in a knot — essential
- 1 tsp salt

**Ginger-spring onion sauce:**
- 3 tbsp finely minced fresh ginger
- 3 tbsp finely sliced spring onion (green parts)
- ½ tsp salt
- ¼ tsp sugar
- 3 tbsp neutral oil (heated until just smoking)

**To serve:**
- Soy sauce or light soy sauce for dipping
- Steamed rice

---

## Method

1. Bring a pot of water to a boil with ginger slices, knotted spring onion, and salt. The pot should be just large enough to submerge the chicken.
2. Add chicken. Return to a boil, then reduce to a very gentle simmer. Cover and cook for 18–22 minutes depending on size.
3. Check doneness: pierce the thickest part — juices should run clear, not pink.
4. Remove chicken and immediately plunge into ice water for 5 minutes. This stops cooking and keeps the skin firm and smooth.
5. While chicken rests, make the sauce: combine minced ginger and spring onion with salt and sugar in a heatproof bowl.
6. Heat oil in a small pan until just smoking. Pour over the ginger and spring onion — it will sizzle loudly. Stir to combine.
7. Slice chicken and arrange on a plate. Serve with the sauce, soy sauce for dipping, and rice cooked in the poaching broth.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Is the sauce punchy enough?** It should hit with ginger heat and fresh onion aroma — taste and add more salt or a few drops of sesame oil if flat.
- **Is the chicken seasoned?** The poaching adds gentle flavour; the dipping sauce and ginger oil carry the seasoning.

---

## Make it yours

- Use the poaching liquid to cook jasmine rice for an incredibly fragrant result.
- Add a small piece of dried tangerine peel to the poaching water for extra fragrance.
- Serve with chilli sauce alongside for those who want heat.

---

## The next day

Store chicken and sauce separately in the fridge for up to 2 days. Slice cold chicken over rice or noodles with the sauce — excellent cold.

---

## Food safety note

Ensure internal temperature reaches 74°C / 165°F. Do not serve undercooked poultry.
