# Dal Tadka

*The most nourishing dish in South Asian home cooking. Cheap, fast, and endlessly comforting.*

---

## About this dish

Dal tadka is made with red or yellow lentils cooked into a thick, spiced soup, then finished with a tarka — a sizzle of hot butter or ghee with cumin, garlic, chilli, and spices poured over the top. The sizzle is theatrical and important: it blooms the spices and adds an incredible fragrance that raw spices in the pot cannot replicate.

## Ingredients

**For the dal:**
- 200g red lentils (masoor dal), rinsed — essential
- 700ml water — essential
- 1 medium tomato, chopped — essential
- ½ onion, finely diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
- ½ tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp salt

**For the tarka:**
- 2 tbsp ghee or butter (or neutral oil for vegan)
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 2 dried red chillies or ½ tsp chilli flakes
- 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- ½ tsp garam masala

**To finish:**
- Fresh coriander, chopped
- Lemon juice

---

## Method

1. Combine rinsed lentils, water, tomato, onion, garlic, ginger, turmeric, and salt in a pot. Bring to a boil.
2. Reduce to a simmer. Cook uncovered for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are completely soft and the mixture is thick and creamy. Add more water if it becomes too thick.
3. Taste and adjust salt.
4. **Make the tarka.** Heat ghee in a small pan over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds — they should sizzle immediately. Add garlic slices and dried chillies. Cook for 30–45 seconds until garlic is golden.
5. Add garam masala, stir once, and immediately pour the entire sizzling tarka over the dal.
6. Stir once or twice, finish with lemon juice and fresh coriander. Serve over basmati rice or with roti.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Too thick?** Add hot water and stir — it should pour off a spoon slowly.
- **Too bland?** More salt, more lemon, or a second small tarka poured over before serving.
- **Needs warmth?** A pinch more chilli in the tarka.

---

## Make it yours

- Add a handful of spinach to the dal in the last 5 minutes.
- Use yellow split peas (chana dal) for a nuttier, firmer version with a longer cook time.
- A spoonful of coconut milk stirred in at the end makes it creamier and milder.

---

## The next day

Dal thickens significantly overnight. Reheat with a splash of water. Freezes well for up to 3 months. Better the second day.
