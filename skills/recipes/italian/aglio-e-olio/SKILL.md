# Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

*The purist's pasta. Four ingredients. One technique. Completely satisfying.*

---

## About this dish

Aglio e olio is a Neapolitan pasta made with nothing but spaghetti, garlic, olive oil, chilli, and parsley. It is one of the most important dishes to master because it teaches the core skill of Italian pasta cookery: using starchy pasta water to emulsify the sauce into something silky rather than greasy.

## Ingredients

**Essential:**
- 200g spaghetti
- 5–6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 5 tbsp good quality extra virgin olive oil
- 1–2 dried chillies or ½ tsp chilli flakes
- Small handful flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- Salt for pasta water

**Optional:**
- Grated parmesan or pecorino (not traditional but delicious)
- Toasted breadcrumbs (pangrattato) instead of cheese

---

## Method

1. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Salt it generously — it should taste like mild seawater.
2. Cook spaghetti for 2 minutes less than the packet instructions (it will finish cooking in the pan).
3. While pasta cooks, warm olive oil in a large pan over medium-low heat. Add garlic slices. Cook very gently — they should turn golden slowly, not brown. About 3–4 minutes. Add chilli flakes.
4. **Reserve at least 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining.**
5. Add drained pasta to the garlic oil. Add a generous splash of pasta water. Toss vigorously over medium heat for 1–2 minutes. The water and oil should emulsify into a glossy, light sauce that coats the pasta.
6. Add more pasta water if it looks dry. Toss in parsley. Taste and season with salt.
7. Serve immediately.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Greasy rather than saucy?** You need more pasta water and more tossing — the starch emulsifies the oil.
- **Garlic bitter?** It was cooked too fast on too high heat. Next time: low and slow.
- **Needs brightness?** A tiny squeeze of lemon lifts the whole dish.

---

## Make it yours

- Add 6 anchovies with the garlic — they melt into the oil and add incredible depth without tasting fishy.
- Top with toasted pangrattato (breadcrumbs fried in olive oil with garlic) instead of cheese.
- Add a handful of cherry tomatoes with the garlic for sweetness.

---

## The next day

Doesn't keep well — pasta absorbs the oil and becomes sticky. Cook only what you eat. Leftover garlic oil is excellent on bread or drizzled over vegetables.
