# Simple Tomato Pasta Sauce

*The most useful sauce to master. Cook it once and use it for everything.*

---

## About this dish

A good tomato sauce made from canned tomatoes is one of the most valuable things a home cook can learn. It takes 25 minutes, uses almost nothing, and can become the base for pasta, pizza, baked eggs, meatballs, aubergine parmigiana, and more. The secret is patience: let it reduce slowly.

## Ingredients

**Essential:**
- 1 × 400g can whole peeled tomatoes (San Marzano or quality brand)
- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- Salt

**Optional but recommended:**
- Small handful fresh basil or a pinch of dried oregano
- ½ tsp sugar (if tomatoes are very acidic)
- Pinch of chilli flakes

---

## Method

1. Warm olive oil over medium-low heat. Add garlic and cook gently for 2 minutes until soft and just golden.
2. Add the canned tomatoes. Crush them with a wooden spoon or your hand as you add them.
3. Add salt, chilli flakes if using. Stir.
4. Simmer uncovered over low-medium heat for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened and reduced, the raw tomato flavour has mellowed, and the oil is visible on the surface.
5. Taste. Add sugar if too acidic. Add more salt if flat.
6. Tear in fresh basil off the heat. Toss with cooked pasta and a splash of pasta water.

---

## Taste like a chef

- **Still tastes raw and sharp?** Needs more time — keep simmering.
- **Too acidic?** A pinch of sugar or a small piece of butter stirred in at the end.
- **Too thick?** Loosen with pasta water, not plain water.
- **Missing depth?** Add a parmesan rind to the sauce while it simmers.

---

## Make it yours

- **Arrabbiata:** Double the chilli and skip the basil.
- **Puttanesca:** Add anchovies, olives, and capers at the start.
- **Marinara with seafood:** Add prawns or clams in the last 3 minutes.

---

## The next day

Keeps in the fridge for 4 days and freezes for 3 months. Make a double batch and freeze in portions — one of the best things you can have on hand.
